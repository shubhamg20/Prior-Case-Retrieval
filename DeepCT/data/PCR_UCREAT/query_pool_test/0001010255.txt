CASE NO Appeal civil of PETITIONER STATE OF RESPONDENT ALLIED CONSTRUCTIONS DATE OF JUDGMENT BENCH KHARE CJ BALAKRISHNAN SINHA JUDGMENT JUDGMENT Supp SCR The following Order of the Court was delivered Under a contract entered into by and between the appellant and the respondent the respondent undertook construction of bridge cum fall at Munda Khera Scape at the estimated cost of Rs Ultimately the matter was referred to an arbitrator The arbitrator gave an award for payment of a sum of Rs The appellant herein filed a petition inter alia on the ground that the arbitrator has misconducted the proceedings inasmuch as the force majeure contained in Clause disentitled the respondent from making any claim which was on account of unprecedented rain The said objection was rejected and the award was made rule of the <ORG> The appellant thereafter filed a first appeal from order before the High <ORG> and the same was dismissed Learned counsel appearing for the appellant reiterated his argument raised before <ORG> We do not find any merit in this contention In absence of such an evidence the arbitrator as well as <ORG> has recorded a finding of fact that the flood which has caused loss to the respondent was not due to the unprecedented rain and therefore Clause of the Agreement was not attracted Any award made by an arbitrator can be set aside only if one or the other term specified in Sections and of the Arbitration Act is attracted It is not a case where it can be said that the arbitrator has misconducted the proceedings It is submitted that an award made by an arbitrator may be wrong either on law or on fact and error of law on the face of it could not nullify an award Section of the Arbitration Act providing for setting aside an award is restrictive in its operation Unless one or the other condition contained in Section is satisfied an award cannot be set aside Even in a case where the award contains reasons the interference therewith would still be not available within the jurisdiction of the <ORG> unless of course the reasons are totally perverse or the judgment is based on a wrong proposition of law The appeal is disposed of in the aforesaid terms
