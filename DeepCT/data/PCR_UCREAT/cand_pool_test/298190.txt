It also ignores the fact that by virtue of Section of the same Act when the land is sold for arrears of rent in respect thereof the purchaser takes free of all encumbrances except such as have been created with the landholder permission or have been created prior to the passing of the Act No precise case under the Estates Land Act has been cited on this point but there is ample authority for a similar proposition with regard to sales for arrears of land revenue due to the <ORG> pending a mortgage suit against the ryot I have no hesitation in holding that the sale by the Collector for arrears of rent of the land on which those arrears were due to the landholder is not affected by a pending suit on a mortgage executed by the ryot who is in default The appeals are therefore dismissed with costs
