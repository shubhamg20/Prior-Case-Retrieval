Counsel appearing on behalf of the Respondents waives service There is no dispute before the <ORG> about the position that these three institutions have been established and are being administered by a religious minority consisting of persons who practice and profess <ORG> Accordingly appeals were filed on th November in order to challenge the <ORG> Resolution of th February The submission which has been urged in support of these petitions is that the <ORG> has furnished no reason whatsoever for declining to accede to the request made by the managements for the enhancement of the management quota <ORG> held that it was open to the University or <ORG> while granting recognition to require a private unaided institution to provide for meritbased selection while giving the management a sufficient discretion in admitting students For instance a certain percentage of the seats can be reserved for admission by the management out of those students who have passed the Common Entrance Test held by itself or by the <ORG> or University and have applied to the college concerned for admission while the rest of the seats may be filled up on the basis of counseling by the <ORG> agency The Court held that the prescription of percentage for this purpose has to be done by <ORG> according to local needs and different percentages can be fixed for minority unaided and non minority unaided and professional colleges Whether the admission of students to minority educational institution whether aided or unaided can be regulated by <ORG> or by the university to which the institution is affiliated While deciding upon this issue <ORG> held that the right to admit students being an essential facet of the right of religious and linguistic minorities to administer educational institutions of their choice under Article of the Constitution <ORG> or the <ORG> may not be entitled to interfere with that right so long as admissions to unaided educational institutions are on a transparent basis and merit is adequately taken care of The Court noted that in the case of non minority professional colleges a certain percentage of seats can be reserved for admission by the management In fixing the percentage for unaided minority professional colleges the <ORG> must keep in mind apart from local needs the interest need of that community in the <ORG> Para of the judgment contains the following observations which must govern the decision making process of the <ORG> It is clarified that different percentage of quota for students to be admitted by the management in each minority or nonminority unaided professional college shall be separately fixed on the basis of their need by the respective State Governments and in case of any dispute as regards fixation of percentage of quota it will be open the management to approach the Committee There is merit in the submission which has been urged on behalf of the Petitioners that the <ORG> having not furnished any indication of reasons in its decision should be requested to reconsider its decision in the light of the principles of law which have been laid down by <ORG> We therefore request the <ORG> to reconsider the matter and in order to facilitate a fresh determination we set aside the impugned decision dated th June
